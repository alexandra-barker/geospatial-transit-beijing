Composition

Hypothesis: The composition of transportation types notably changes based on the time of day, day of the week, and month of the year, with the majority of activity during the early morning, work week, and summer months.

While time impacts travel activity in both quantity and duration as soon in Hypothesis 1, composition of these travel trends can also provide critical insights. For example, is there more walking during the summer months and/or during daylight hours? With Beijing being one of the most congested places in the world, looking at how transportation changes over these time intervals can provide information that can support decision-making for city planners (e.g., when is the most optimal time to do construction on a sidewalk vs. a road vs. the subway system).

These visualizations show how the composition of activity based on analysis of each GPS recording changes for various time intervals of analysis. To show relativity and absolute quantity, each visualization can be displayed in absolute numbers and percentage of the overall activity recordings for that time segment. To update the visualizations, click on the different options to change both the radial and line charts.
