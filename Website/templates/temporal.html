Temporal

Hypothesis: Transportation activities in both duration and quantity change notably based on time, with the majority of activity concentrated in the summer months and with the longest commutes ending during end-of-day hours.

Experiential analysis suggests that most transportation changes based off the time interval for evaluation. For example, Monday through Friday, it may be generally accepted that there is a greater number of travelers in the morning and in the late afternoon due to commutes to and from work. Similarly, the duration of that travel in time may change based on time as well with greater commutes ending mid or late day for travelers that have begun their journey in the morning.
