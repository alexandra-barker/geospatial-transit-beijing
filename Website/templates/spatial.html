Hypothesis: Spatial

Commuting channels and most common transit methods will be denser and more varied in the inner city and during national holidays.

The frequency of travel increases in the summer months and early fall, when there are major national holidays. The mid-autumn festival takes place in September and China's National Day (equivalent to the 4th of July) is in October. Chinese employers typically only provide 5-10 days of paid time off, so for many Chinese workers, public holidays are opportunities for them to have dedicated time away from work and see family.

There are also concentrated travel patterns in the inner city, where workers' offices are. It's easy to see the commercial districts due to method of transportation density. There is significant walking and bicycle activity near Beijing's large universities and largest public parks. There is also significant bicycle activity near Beijing's Olympic Village and stadium.
